---
title: Topological Charges in Closed Spacetimes and Homotopy of Lorentzian Metrics
date: 2026-07-30T13:30-03:00
speaker: 
    - name: Alexandre Mendonça
---
Wheeler's geometrodynamics proposes, amongst other concepts, that electric charge can emerge from the topological and geometrical properties of a spacetime devoid of matter. This notion is made rigorous through De Rham cohomology, which allows electric charge to be described as a linear functional over the second homology group of the spacetime.

Following Unruh's unpublished PhD thesis, the concept of charge without charge is extended to define geometrodynamical charges associated to closed differential forms constructed from the metric tensor and its derivatives only, without imposing field equations on the metric. By analyzing the homotopy classes of the metric, viewed as a map into the bundle of Lorentzian metrics, we verify if such charges can exist in closed submanifolds of a four dimensional spacetime.
