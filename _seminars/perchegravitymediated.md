Recent experimental proposals consider two test masses are in superpositions of paths with gravity being the only relevant interaction between them. The predictions for the experiment are different for different theories of the interaction between gravity and quantized matter, allowing one to rule out theories such as semiclassical gravity if entanglement is seen between the masses. Although the experiment confirms that the gravitational field can mediate entanglement, additional assumptions are necessary to confirm non-classical behaviour of the gravitational field. In this talk we will discuss some of the necessary assumptions that can be used to conclude that the gravitational field admits an effective description as a non-classical field, as well as which regimes of the experiment could be used to test whether this is the case.
